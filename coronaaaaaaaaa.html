MY DAILY LIFE DURING MCO!

Hello everyone, thanks for checking out my page.

People keep approaching me on social media, they asked me to make a video of my daily life while MCO is still going on. Yes video is coming out on my YouTbe channel on this Thursday so make sure to check it out. But i have a thought that i want to post something here (on my page) today. i know this is kinda weird to put down some weird essay like this on my main page especially for business page like this. Well, to make it simple, i've been doing the same things since day one of MCO. Keep repeating the same routine, lets call it "MR.Corona routine".

It feels like we’re a community but we are separated by walls. I realised that I didn’t want to dwell on suffering. I've started posting videos on social media to share life hacks – some useful (like tips for folding clothes in three seconds) and some not so conventionally helpful (how to make a slingshot from a rubber band). It’s one of the waysi have learned to cope on lockdown, as just one of thousands of students in the campus who are now isolating in university halls and student accommodation.

Sometimes i have to get outside of my room to exercise. Get out and do a sensible amount of exercise in a single day. I don’t make the mistake of thinking i can exercise myself to sleep. Things like this will make me relieve some of the stresses that would otherwise accumulate through a confined environment. Exercise doesn’t have to be a workout. “It can mean moderate walking. It can simply mean being outdoors, raising your heart rate at about 50%,” they said so i did HAHAHAHAHA.

What else do you expect for me to do in my daily life during MCO?? I am a game creator and also a pro-gamer. Game will be included in my daily routine.

Thanks for checking out my page once again and thank you for reading my silly daily diaries while MCO. Stay Safe and please stay at home!

View Profile

I’m a University student of UITM cawangan Samarahan Sarawak, who loves clean, simple & unique design. I also enjoy playing music

Together with a talented team of UITM students and alumni, Daren Dave is creating an interactive, short story-like video game, set for release later this summer. Daren was inspired by Edgar Allan Poe’s single-sitting story format, and hopes to bring the same richness to a video game that can be played in less than two hours. As they move on from the UITM Malaysia, most recent graduates are preparing to face a whole new world. Daren Dave has actually created one. Daren is the creative director of Corona Lantern Games and spent part of her studying years working on the side to develop an interactive, short story-style video game called “Gray Skies, Dark Waters.”

“I was inspired by Edgar Allan Poe’s notion that the ideal story can be read in a single sitting,” Daren said. “He was the master of the short story and I wanted to make a game that could be played in a single sitting, so between one and two hours.”
